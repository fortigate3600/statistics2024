Relazione tra le medie

The mean of the sample means calculated is quite close to the theoretical mean of the parent distribution, with a discrepancy that results in a slight underestimation. This difference, although not significant, could be attributed to various factors, such as the limited sample size or the possible presence of a systematic bias during the sampling process. In an ideal context, one would expect the mean of the sample means to converge to the theoretical mean, following the Central Limit Theorem. However, even small deviations can occur in practice due to statistical fluctuations and the specific structure of the sampling method used.

Regarding the variance, once again, the observed difference can be explained by the limitations of sample size and the intrinsic variability in variance estimates. The general trend, however, confirms that the sample variances provide a reasonable estimate of the theoretical variance, demonstrating the validity of the sampling method employed.
